About Me

Thomas Mawora Mwakudisa is a Lecturer of Statistics in the Department of Statistics and Actuarial Science at Maseno University. He is also a co-founder of the Maseno University Laboratory for Interdisciplinary Statistical Analysis (MU-LISA). Thomas specializes in handling data, research methodology, and providing consulting services in areas such as research methodology advice, data analysis, data management, and data science projects. He holds a PhD in Applied Statistics. In his personal life, Thomas finds inspiration in the Bible and the book Bounce. His motivation is to empower the next generation of statisticians to use data to make a positive impact on society. Thomas has published research on time series analysis from his PhD work and has collaborated with PISTAR on several paper writing sessions. He is currently serving as the Patron to the Statistical Association of Maseno University (SASMU).

My Projects

Mulisa-Origin
Co-Founders-Dr.Mawora & Dr.Joyce

Established in August 2019, the Maseno University Laboratory for Interdisciplinary Statistical Analysis (MU-LISA) is dedicated to empowering learners to use statistical tools and methods for sound analysis across multidisciplinary research projects.

Mulisa-Goals

MU-LISA's primary goals are to train participants in statistical software and analytical techniques, provide a conducive environment for developing and conducting cross-disciplinary research, and enable participants to publish in refereed journals.

Mulisa-Mission

In pursuit of its mission, MU-LISA offers regular training sessions, tailored workshops, and attachment/internship opportunities for students. The lab has provided training to over 200 students and conducted more than 30 software-related sessions through collaborations with student societies.
